Research Foundation

Why Maple Bridge

Maple Bridge was selected because the site attracts many visitors but does not always support meaningful understanding.

The current motivation draft identifies Maple Bridge in the Suzhou Grand Canal context as a location where visitor volume is likely to expose practical barriers. First-time tourists may not know where popular viewpoints are located, older adults may face orientation difficulties, and international visitors may encounter language barriers.

The same draft also suggests that visitors can struggle to build a coherent understanding of the attraction, with the experience narrowing into surface-level “photo-only” engagement rather than deeper cultural appreciation. This framing is used here as the current rationale for choosing Maple Bridge, not yet as a validated research finding.

Project Motivation

The current motivation centers on lowering the threshold for cultural engagement through a web-based interactive platform.

The local draft proposes a web-based online interactive platform as an appropriate response. Its direction is not to rigidify traditional culture, but to support access through lightweight interactions that make exploration easier and calmer for different kinds of visitors.

Examples named in the draft include check-in challenges, lightweight story guidance, and simple online interactions based on traditional ritual elements. It also mentions more practical support such as map functions, multilingual switching, and visitor photo publication. Together, these indicate a motivation that combines cultural storytelling with inclusive navigation support.
