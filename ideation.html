Early Ideas

The ideation process began by opening up multiple directions before selecting a stable structure.

At this stage, the project needed to explore more than one possible way to present Fengqiao and Maple Bridge digitally. The purpose of this phase was not to settle early on the first workable page layout, but to make room for different interaction models, different navigation depths, and different balances between storytelling and utility.

The current evidence shows that the team considered content complexity, interaction needs, and user orientation as major constraints. This makes ideation here less about decorative styling and more about how the system should be structured to support both quick browsing and deeper exploration.

What this stage needed to answer

  • How much content should stay on one page
  • How deeply interaction features should be separated
  • How to balance cultural storytelling with practical guidance
  • How to avoid clutter while preserving discoverability

Design Alternatives

Three structural alternatives were compared before the final direction was selected.

The comparison text below is grounded in the local alternative-analysis document and is organized to foreground trade-offs rather than flattening all options into neutral summaries.

Alternative 01

One-page website

Strength: Simple, continuous, and suited to lightweight storytelling.

Risk: Interactive functions and dense content would overcrowd the page and raise cognitive load.

Trade-off: Gains narrative continuity but loses clarity once features expand.

Alternative 02

Fully multi-page website

Strength: Separates content clearly and allows more layout freedom for different topics.

Risk: Can feel fragmented, increase switching cost, and weaken overall coherence.

Trade-off: Gains modular separation but risks breaking browsing flow and orientation.

Alternative 03

One main page with several subpages

Strength: Balances quick overview with deeper exploration of selected tasks and content.

Risk: Still requires careful grouping and stable navigation to avoid confusion.

Trade-off: Preserves identity at the top level while supporting depth where needed.

Comparison Of Candidate Structures

The project compared candidate structures by how well they balanced clarity, depth, and interaction needs.

Candidate structure What it supports well Main trade-off Fit for Maple Bridge
One-page website Continuous storytelling and quick linear browsing Becomes crowded once navigation, route tools, poem interaction, elder mode, and other features accumulate Too shallow for the project’s interaction complexity
Fully multi-page website Strong content separation and flexibility per section Higher switching cost and weaker sense of one coherent experience Usable, but risks fragmentation for first-time users
Main page + subpages Fast overview on the homepage plus deeper task support on focused pages Requires disciplined information grouping and consistent navigation Best fit for combining cultural identity with scalable interaction depth

Why The Final Direction Was Selected

The chosen direction was the main-page-plus-subpages structure because it balanced overview and depth more effectively than the alternatives.

According to the comparison text, the selected direction works because the homepage can establish identity, highlights, and entry points quickly, while subpages can carry denser interactions such as route recommendation, poem-based interpretation, and other feature groups without overwhelming the user.

This decision was not only about visual layout. It was also about breadth and depth: keeping the overall system legible while letting specific functions become more focused where necessary. The selected structure was also described as more scalable for future additions, which makes it a stronger long-term fit than a single scrolling page.

Source: Comparison of Alternatives.txt

  • Supports quick overview on the homepage
  • Allows deeper task-focused subpages
  • Reduces clutter compared with a one-page system
  • Preserves more coherence than a fully separated multi-page system
  • Scales more safely as features expand
